The firehose effect

  • Overstimulation → scattered attention. Constant novelty trains the brain to expect interruption.
  • Comparison loops → lower self‑worth. Public metrics and highlight reels skew what “normal” looks like.
  • Intermittent rewards → compulsive checking. The refresh roulette keeps you pulling the lever.

Ethical design

Built for human connection, not doomscrolling

Weekly feed refresh

One shared drop (Fridays, 6:00 PM Central) breaks the habit loop and turns sharing into a moment, not a reflex.

No algorithmically served content

Your timeline shows only people you follow. No explore page. No injected profiles. No endless “for you.”

No performative metrics

No public like or follower counts. Celebrate photos, not scores.

Tight privacy controls

Quiet defaults, clear account‑level controls, opt‑in notifications, and easy export/deletion.

A healthier relationship with sharing

Silence is allowed. Boredom is allowed. We want technology that leaves room for friendship, craft, and attention to the world right in front of you.

Pixeldrop won’t fix everything. But the right friction points help you choose how you spend your time — not have that choice made for you.
